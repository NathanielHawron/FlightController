Intro

Date: 06/20/2026

For this project, I wanted to create a VTOL and a custom flight controller. In my first year of university, I joined the Aerospace club, where I first learned about VTOLs. Someone from the club had spent he summer working on a foam tricopter VTOL (where the forward rotors tilt forwards to go into fixed wing flight, while the rear rotor is used only to hover in tricopter mode). Unfortunately, it was unable to transition correctly, and ended up crashing, before it was given a funeral in a bonfire (with all the electronics removed of course).

After that, we were discussing ideas for the AEAC competition, and it seemed like a VTOL would be perfect for the given tasks, however it was too complicated for us to attempt, and we went with a helicopter instead. I did some minor research into VTOLs at this time, but learned that the flight control software wasn't really built for that, and it was mostly a hacky method of combining fixed wing and quatcopter flight modes.

I wasn't too involved with the club anymore until the start of my fourth year, where we were tasked with carrying a heavy payload as fast as possible over the longest distance possible. We ended up going with a quadcopter, which is what we were familliar with, although on paper, a VTOL would be a good solution. This time, I went to competition, and saw why most teams are afraid of VTOLs: they are complicated and tend to have issues (and there wasn't too much space, so it would need to transition quickly and have a tight turning radius, making multicopters seem much better).

However, it seemed like a fun challenge to attempt, and since I had just taken a control systems course and learned how to design PCBs, so I wanted to also try my hand at designing the flight controller and flight software as well. Additionally, I met some people at competition that were also interested in custom flight controllers.
